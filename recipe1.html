Sambaar

Description

Sambar is a hearty South Indian lentil and mixed vegetable stew known for its robust flavors from tamarind, aromatic spices, and fresh sambar powder. This traditional recipe features toor dal cooked until mushy, combined with vegetables like drumsticks, carrots, okra, onions, and tomatoes, simmered with a special spice blend for an authentic taste. It's nutrient-dense, protein-packed, and pairs perfectly with rice, idli, dosa, or vada, making it a staple in South Indian homes

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • cups water (to cook dal)
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons tamarind or tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery (optional)
  • ¾ tablespoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ to ¾ teaspoon red chilli powder (optional)
  • ¼ cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Vegetables: Onion,Tomato,Carrot,Brinjal,Lady's Finger,Bottle Guard etc
  • Sambaar powder

Steps

  1. Wash ¾ cup toor dal until water runs clear. Pressure cook with 2 cups water for 2-4 whistles (or 9 mins in Instant Pot) until mushy. Mash well with a ladle.
  2. Dry roast for sambar powder: red chilies, chana dal, urad dal until golden; add coriander seeds until aromatic; roast methi then cumin separately. Cool and grind to powder.
  3. Rinse and chop vegetables: drumsticks to 2-inch pieces, okra to 1-inch, dice onion, tomato, carrot. Heat 1 tsp oil, sauté onions 1 min, add veggies 2 mins, stir in chili powder (optional).
  4. Add 4 cups water, boil until veggies are al dente. Soak tamarind and jaggery in ¼ cup hot water separately.
  5. Add turmeric, sambar powder, salt. Cook 3-5 mins until veggies soften. Squeeze and filter tamarind water, add to pot.
  6. Stir in mashed dal, adjust salt/tamarind/water for consistency. Boil gently, add coriander leaves.
  7. For tempering: Heat ghee/oil, add mustard, cumin, methi, red chilies; when popping, add curry leaves and hing. Pour over simmering sambar, simmer 2-3 mins.
  8. Serve hot with rice, idli, or dosa.
